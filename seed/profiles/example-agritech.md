# Company description (founder's own words — onboarding input)

We are **TerraSense**, an agritech startup based in Sfax. We are building a smart
irrigation platform that uses low-cost IoT soil sensors and an AI model to tell
farmers exactly when and how much to water, cutting water use by up to 40% and
increasing yields. Tunisia is running out of water and our olive and vegetable
farmers waste enormous amounts — this is a huge market and we are the team to win
it.

We are a registered SARL (incorporated 7 months ago) with three co-founders: a
agronomist, an embedded-systems engineer, and myself (I handle business). We have
built a working prototype of the sensor and a mobile app dashboard, and we have
installed it on **two pilot farms** owned by family contacts who love it. We are
**ready to raise a seed round of 800,000 TND** to scale manufacturing and hire a
sales team, and we believe a 5,000,000 TND valuation is fair given the size of the
opportunity. We have a pitch deck and we are talking to a few investors. We think
we are basically fundraising-ready.

# Why the reality is Structuration (not Fundraising) — diagnosis notes

This profile is the demo's teaching case: a confident founder whose self-assessment
("fundraising-ready") outruns the evidence. An honest diagnosis lands at
**Structuration**, and the Signals must reflect the gaps, not the ambition.

**What is real (genuine strengths):**

- Incorporated (SARL) with a complete, credible founding team (domain + technical
  + business). — supports Innovation, Scalability fundamentals.
- A working hardware + app **prototype** exists and is demoable. — supports
  product/service readiness.
- A real, urgent problem (water scarcity in Tunisian agriculture) and a plausible
  large market. — supports Market sizing.
- A clear environmental thesis (40% water reduction). — supports the Green Signal.

**What is missing (caps the Signals via gated aggregation):**

- **No customer validation.** "Two pilot farms owned by family contacts" are not
  arm's-length customers — no paying users, no signed LOIs, no retention data.
  The Market lens's *fundamental* (customer validation evidence) is weak → the
  Market Signal must be **floor-triggered** and capped.
- **No revenue model evidence.** No pricing tested, no willingness-to-pay data, no
  paying customer. The viability lens's *fundamental* (revenue model & pricing
  coherence) is weak → the Green/viability composite and Commercial Offer are
  capped.
- **No unit economics.** No CAC, no margin on the hardware, no understanding of
  manufacturing cost at scale — yet the plan is to "scale manufacturing".
- **Manual / hardware dependency.** Each install is currently a hands-on hardware
  deployment; scalability without linear cost is unproven → the feasibility lens's
  *fundamental* (scalability & manual dependency) is weak.
- **Valuation/ask not grounded.** An 800k TND raise at a 5M TND valuation is
  asserted from market size, not from traction — a classic Structuration-stage
  over-reach.

**Honest stage call:** `Structuration` — incorporated and building, prototype in
hand, but pre-validation and pre-revenue. The right next moves are validation and
non-dilutive support, **not** an equity seed round yet.

**Stage-appropriate, KB-grounded next actions (what the scorer/advisors should
generate):**

1. Run arm's-length **customer-discovery interviews** with 8–10 unrelated farmers
   and convert at least one into a **paid pilot or signed LOI**. (kb-product —
   Customer Discovery / MVP Validation.)
2. Pursue the **Startup Act Label** to unlock founder salary support and tax
   relief while pre-revenue. (kb-fundraising — Startup Act Labelisation.)
3. Apply for **non-dilutive R&D / innovation support** (PIRD via APII) and a
   **green / energy-efficiency** scheme (ANME / FTE, SwitchMed) to fund the
   prototype-to-product step without diluting at a premature valuation.
   (kb-product PIRD; kb-green ANME, SwitchMed.)
4. Hold off on the equity seed round until there is validated revenue and unit
   economics; then engage Flat6Labs Tunis / Carthage Business Angels / a SICAR.
   (kb-tunisia, kb-fundraising.)
