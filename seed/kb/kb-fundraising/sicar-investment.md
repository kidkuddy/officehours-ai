# SICAR — Sociétés d'Investissement à Capital Risque

**What it is.** SICARs are Tunisia's regulated venture-capital / private-equity
investment companies. They take minority equity (and quasi-equity such as
convertible accounts) in unlisted Tunisian companies, typically with a planned
exit (buy-back, sale, or IPO) over several years. Many SICARs manage funds that
benefit from fiscal incentives and from the Startup Act guarantee, channelling
both private and public money into growth-stage and structured early-stage firms.

**Who qualifies.** Incorporated Tunisian companies with growth potential and a
credible path to value creation and exit. Earlier-stage startups generally need
the Startup Label and demonstrable traction; SICARs invest in equity, so a clean
cap table and clear governance matter.

**How to apply.** Approach SICAR managers directly (e.g. via the Tunisian VC
association ATIC) with a pitch deck, business plan, and financials; the round is
then structured and negotiated, often alongside the Startup Act guarantee.

**Source.** https://www.atic.org.tn (Association Tunisienne des Investisseurs en Capital)
