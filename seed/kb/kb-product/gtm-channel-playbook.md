# Go-to-Market — Channel Selection & Traction Channels

**What it is.** A structured way to find a repeatable acquisition channel, drawing
on the "Bullseye" framework (Weinberg & Mares, *Traction*) which lists ~19
channels — content/SEO, SEM, social & display ads, offline ads, PR, viral/referral,
email, engineering-as-marketing, content partnerships, community building,
business development, direct sales, affiliate, existing platforms, trade shows,
offline events, speaking, and more. The method: brainstorm every channel, rank
them, test the top three cheaply, then double down on the one that works.

**Who qualifies / when to use.** Founders at Market Validation → Launch Planning
who are converting product into repeatable revenue, and anyone whose Market or
Commercial Offer Signal is weak on a proven, repeatable acquisition motion.

**How to apply.** List candidate channels; for the top three, design a small,
cheap, time-boxed test with a clear cost-per-acquisition target; keep the channel
that produces customers at an acceptable CAC and is repeatable beyond founder
hustle.

**Source.** *Traction* (Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares).
