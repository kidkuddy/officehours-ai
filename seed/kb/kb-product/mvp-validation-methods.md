# MVP & Cheap Validation Methods

**What it is.** A toolkit of low-cost experiments to test demand before building
the full product. Common patterns: the **landing-page / smoke test** (measure
sign-ups or pre-orders for a value proposition), the **concierge MVP** (deliver
the service manually to the first users), the **Wizard-of-Oz MVP** (a working
front end with humans behind the scenes), and the **pre-sale** (collect payment or
binding LOIs before delivery). The goal is validated learning per dollar, not a
polished product.

**Who qualifies / when to use.** Founders at Ideation/Market Validation/
Structuration who need evidence that customers want and will pay for the solution,
and want to avoid building the wrong thing. Directly raises the Commercial Offer
and Market Signals when it produces paying or committed users.

**How to apply.** Pick the single riskiest assumption; choose the cheapest MVP that
can test it; define a clear success metric and threshold up front; run it
time-boxed (1–2 weeks); decide pivot-or-persevere on the data.

**Source.** *The Lean Startup* (Eric Ries) — http://theleanstartup.com
