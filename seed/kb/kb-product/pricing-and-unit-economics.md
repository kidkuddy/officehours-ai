# Pricing Strategy & Unit Economics

**What it is.** The fundamentals of making the business model viable. **Pricing**
is a positioning and value-capture decision: value-based pricing (price to the
value delivered) generally beats cost-plus for differentiated products. **Unit
economics** measure whether each customer is profitable: key metrics are CAC
(customer acquisition cost), LTV (lifetime value), gross margin, payback period,
and the LTV:CAC ratio (a common health benchmark is ≥ 3:1 with payback under ~12
months). A business can have strong demand and still fail if the unit economics
don't work.

**Who qualifies / when to use.** Founders preparing to raise (Fundraising) or
scale (Growth), and any founder whose Commercial Offer or Scalability Signal is
weak on "revenue model coherence" or "unit economics". Investors and the BFPME /
SICAR committees scrutinize these directly.

**How to apply.** Estimate CAC per channel from real spend; estimate LTV from price,
margin, and retention; compute LTV:CAC and payback; test willingness to pay with
real offers (not surveys); iterate pricing toward value capture.

**Source.** Standard startup-finance practice; *Lean Analytics* (Croll & Yoskovitz).
