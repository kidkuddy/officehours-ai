# Customer Discovery — The Mom Test & Validation Interviews

**What it is.** A practical method (popularized by Rob Fitzpatrick's *The Mom
Test* and Steve Blank's Customer Development) for getting truthful signal from
customer conversations. The core rule: talk about the customer's life and past
behaviour, not your idea. Ask about specific problems they've actually had, what
they did about them, and what it cost them — never "would you use this?" Good
discovery produces evidence (real problems, current workarounds, willingness to
pay), not compliments.

**Who qualifies / when to use.** Any founder at Ideation or Market Validation who
needs to confirm a problem is real and painful before building. Especially
critical when the Market Signal's "customer validation evidence" sub-criterion is
weak.

**How to apply.** Define one riskiest assumption; recruit 5–10 people in the target
segment; run 20-minute conversations about their past behaviour; log concrete facts
(not opinions); look for problems people already spend time or money solving. A
signed letter of intent (LOI) or a pre-payment is the strongest validation.

**Source.** *The Mom Test* (Rob Fitzpatrick); Steve Blank, Customer Development —
https://steveblank.com
