# Product-Market Fit — Signals & Metrics

**What it is.** Frameworks for knowing whether you've reached product-market fit
(PMF). Qualitative: Sean Ellis's **"very disappointed" survey** (PMF is plausible
when ≥ 40% of users would be "very disappointed" if they could no longer use the
product). Quantitative: strong **retention curves that flatten** (a stable cohort
keeps using the product), organic word-of-mouth growth, and improving engagement.
Before PMF, the priority is finding it; after PMF, the priority shifts to scaling
acquisition.

**Who qualifies / when to use.** Founders at Market Validation → Launch Planning
deciding whether to scale, and anyone whose Market Signal hinges on retention
evidence. Helps separate real traction from vanity metrics (signups, downloads).

**How to apply.** Instrument activation and retention by cohort; run the Sean Ellis
survey once you have enough active users; treat a flattening retention curve as the
core PMF signal; do not scale spend until retention holds.

**Source.** Sean Ellis (PMF survey); Brian Balfour / Reforge on retention.
