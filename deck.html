The Concept

Real office hours — with specialist Advisors.

  • A founder books a session with an Advisor — Product, Go-to-Market, Fundraising, Pitch — and pitches their startup.
  • Each Advisor is an AI agent that reasons from the company's Dossier: its Data Room (deck, BP, financials), its history, and its current Signals.
  • Sessions produce Action Items, tracked in the Logbook and carried into the next session as context.
OfficeHours.ai05
How It Works

Pitch. Get feedback. Earn the next room.

  1. Founder starts a session and pitches the Advisor. Materials upload up front, later, or mid-conversation.
  2. The Advisor probes and gives feedback. Takeaways, Action Items, and Signals are recorded and monitored.
  3. Advisors unlock as the startup earns it — you start with the Product Advisor; Go-to-Market stays locked until the problem is validated.
  4. Founder works the items; the Logbook tracks progress; Signals re-evaluate after each session.
OfficeHours.ai06
Core AI Mechanism

Every Advisor runs the same four-step engine.

Step 01
Read
Ingests the Dossier — deck, business plan, financials, and past sessions.
Step 02
Diagnose
Places the startup at a maturity stage, tied to the evidence behind it.
Step 03
Score
Computes the Signals across weighted criteria, with a per-criterion breakdown.
Step 04
Orient
Turns each gap into an Action Item matched to a real program, with the source attached.

Advisors draw on admin-managed Playbooks and a catalogue of national & international support and financing programs.

OfficeHours.ai08
The Scoring Model

Signals, built on the stress-test framework.

Desirability.
  • Problem-fit defined
  • Customer validation
  • Market size & competition
Feasibility.
  • Tech score
  • Product readiness
  • Scalability & manual dependency
Viability.
  • Revenue model & pricing
  • Unit economics
  • Sustainability & SDG alignment

When a fundamental dimension scores low, it caps the whole result. Strong scores elsewhere cannot lift it back up.
