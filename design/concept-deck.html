OfficeHours.ai
How the diagnostic, scoring & orientation engine works.
OfficeHours.ai01
The problem

Founders can't see where they actually stand.

  • Self-perception ≠ reality. Founders claim to be "financing-ready" with no validated model — and orientation tools just reflect that inaccurate self-image back.
  • Tools answer, they don't diagnose. Static guides and generic chatbots give information, never a structured assessment of where a project stands.
  • Resources are fragmented & generic. Programs, funding, and procedures aren't matched to a specific project profile.
  • The outcome: of 1,000+ startups under the Startup Act, fewer than 10 reach VC-grade success.
OfficeHours.ai02
The idea

One profile at the center. Three intelligences around it.

A founder describes their company once; that profile becomes the spine. A diagnostic engine classifies it, an explainable scoring engine evaluates it, and a grounded orientation engine points to real next steps. The differentiator is that the three interact — they don't just coexist as panels.

OfficeHours.ai03
The product

It works like real office hours.

Founders hold sessions with specialist AI Advisors that have already read their file. Each session ends with tracked next steps.

Advisor — an AI agent specialized in one domain (Product, GTM, Fundraising, Pitch…).
Signals — the multi-dimensional readiness scores, re-evaluated each session.
Office Hours — a working session; the advisor opens and leads.
Action Items — next steps, each grounded in a real program.
Profile — the living file: data room, history, current signals.
Logbook — the timeline of stage changes, scores, and actions.

OfficeHours.ai05
Engine 1 · Diagnosis

A six-stage maturity taxonomy, classified from evidence.

PhaseStageThe question it answers
Discover1 · IdeationIs there a clearly defined problem worth solving?
Discover2 · Market ValidationIs there evidence real customers want this?
Validate3 · StructurationIs the business structured (legal, team, model)?
Validate4 · Launch PlanningIs the offer ready to go to market?
Scale5 · FundraisingIs it investable on its fundamentals?
Scale6 · GrowthIs growth repeatable without linear cost?

Every classification is tied to the data points behind it. To advance, a startup passes a stage-gate — the gate opens on evidence, not self-assessment.

OfficeHours.ai07
Scoring method

Composite scores are not averages.

Each Signal decomposes into weighted sub-criteria with a per-criterion contribution and a plain-language reason. When a fundamental sub-criterion falls below a floor, it caps the whole score — strong scores elsewhere can't paper over a missing fundamental.

F = 2.0  ·  composite = min( weighted_mean , fundamental_score + 0.5 )
  • Anomaly detection — flags contradictions (high claimed traction, zero validation).
  • Improvement guidance — names the highest-leverage gap per Signal + a concrete action.
OfficeHours.ai08
Engine 3 · Orientation

Every recommendation is grounded in a real program.

  • Real knowledge base — APII, BFPME, BTS, Startup Act, ANPE, incubators, plus AFD / EU / UNDP funding, ingested from heterogeneous docs.
  • Retrieval is traceable — every recommended resource cites its source; the assistant never names a program from memory.
  • Roadmap, not a list — ordered action items with rationale and time horizons (immediate / short / medium).

OfficeHours.ai10
Cross-module integration

A gap in one engine drives the others.

A diagnostic gap (missing market validation) triggers retrieval of programs that address it; a low sub-score surfaces a targeted roadmap action; the advisor's reply references the founder's actual stage, Signals, and cited sources. The shared profile is the spine that connects them.

OfficeHours.ai11
Explainability

Every output is traceable.

  • Why this stage — the classification links to the specific evidence behind it.
  • Why this score — per-criterion contributions, weights, a written rationale, and a CAPPED badge when a fundamental floors it.
  • Why this recommendation — it points back to a named program in the knowledge base.
  • What we don't know — incomplete profiles surface uncertainty instead of guessing.
OfficeHours.ai12
Evaluation

We measure the diagnosis, not just the vibe.

  • Protocol — a labeled set of founder profiles run end-to-end through the real diagnoser.
  • Metric — maturity-stage classification accuracy (exact and within-one-stage), plus Signal consistency across repeated inputs.
  • Edge cases — incomplete or contradictory profiles are handled gracefully.

Under the hood

The stack.

Headless tool-using agents, provider switchable by env. The agent drives a CLI to read context and persist results.
DATA + RAGPostgres
One store for profiles, signals, sessions, and a full-text retrieval index over the program knowledge base.

Go API + React UI, the whole system in docker-compose. FR/AR-ready for the local population.
